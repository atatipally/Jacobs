BCD Travel

  • All travel arrangements should be booked through the corporate travel company, BCD Travel.

TRAVEL AUTHORIZATION FORMS

Travel Authorization are mandatory for all expenses (billable or non-billable/travel or non-travel) that are charged to U.S. Federal Projects. Travel Authorization forms must be attached to each expense report submitted for payment.

BUSINESS PURPOSE FOR TRAVEL/EXPENSE

  • Sufficient detail should be provided so that anyone, at any time, can determine why travel was required, what work was done and who was visited.
  • The business reason/need should be provided all purchases including non-travel related items made via expense reports.

RECEIPTS

  • Receipts are mandatory, regardless of amount, for airline transactions, car rental and lodging. Receipts are also mandatory for any one item in excess of $75. Receipts are recommended for meetings/entertainment, memberships, office supplies, professional dues/licenses, publications/periodicals, seminars/conferences, and training.

AIRFARE

  • Airline ticket/invoice is required. It should detail amount, class, form of payment, and travel dates. Airfare will not be reimbursed without an original receipt. BCD Travel passenger itinerary provides all of this information.
  • Coach class airfare is expected. First/business class airfare should not be booked without prior approval from a Group or Senior Vice President. An explanation of the reason for using anything other than coach should be included.
  • Please attach the receipt for BCD Travel service fee.
  • Please insure that the ticket and expense report travel dates correspond.
  • Please attach the receipt for any cost associated with, exchanges, refunds, voids and lost tickets. Provide an explanation of the circumstances that made the change necessary.
  • Unused airline tickets/vouchers issued by BCD must be returned immediately. Do not exchange tickets at the airport unless instructed to do so.
  • The purchase of airline tickets at the airport is not authorized except in emergency situations a detailed account is necessary.
  • Company paid spouse/family travel must be authorized specifically on a travel authorization in advance.

LODGING

  • Original receipts for lodging are required and must be attached to each expense report. The receipt should contain the name, city and state of the hotel in addition to itemized individual charges such as daily room rate, taxes, parking, meals and meals.
  • Reservations should be made through BCD Travel and not directly with the hotel.
  • All reservations are guaranteed for late arrival.
  • Lodging requires itemization of charges per day. List room and associated tax amounts separately.
  • Lodging expenses shall be considered reasonable only to the extent that they do not exceed the daily maximum Government Per Diem rate in effect at the time of travel when charged to FAR applicable contract or overhead.

RENTAL CARS

  • Reservations for cars should be booked through BCD Travel.
  • Receipts for rental cars are required and must be attached to each expense report. The receipt should contain sufficient detail in order to determine dates of travel, class of auto, and method of payment.
  • HERTZ is Jacobs preferred vendor; National and Enterprise are additional approved vendors.
  • Please provide an explanation/business reason for the rental of any class of car other than an intermediate or compact. For example, a larger car was necessary because you had more then four people, excess equipment or if weather or field conditions dictate.
  • Always try to refuel before returning the auto.
  • Decline LDW and Liability insurance, when renting from the preferred provider in the U.S. or from participating locations in Western Europe.

PRIVATE AUTOMOBILE USE

  • Reimbursement will be made at the current Jacobs mileage rate in effect at the time of travel.
  • Please provide the number of miles driven and the starting and ending points of the trip. Provide the exact city and state for the from/to location entry. Avoid "home to airport" for example; use "Oak Ridge TN to Alcoa TN".
  • The corporate agreements with HERTZ allows for unlimited miles. On longer trips, consideration should be given to renting vs. driving a personal car.

MEALS and INCIDENTIALS

  • Meals during travel should be listed by day.
  • Meal expenses shall be considered reasonable only to the extent that they do not exceed the daily maximum Government Per Diem rate in effect at the time of travel when charged to FAR applicable contract or overhead.

BUSINESS MEETING MEALS

  • Receipts are recommended for all amounts but are required for amount over $75.
  • A detailed business reason/purpose for the meal is required. A short description of "meeting" or "business lunch" is considered insufficient.
  • A list of who attended the business meal and their affiliation (title, company).
  • Identify which meal of the day it was - breakfast, lunch or dinner.
  • Identify any alcohol included on the bill.
  • Always distinguish between business meals and entertainment.

BUSINESS ENTERTAINMENT

  • Defined as all cost of amusement, diversion, social activities and any directly associated cost.
  • Receipts are recommended for all business entertainment expenses regardless of the amount but are required for amounts over $75.
  • A list of attendee's names and titles (i.e. JEG personnel, clients, prospective clients).
  • A description of the reason for entertainment.
  • Identify any alcohol included on the bill.

TEMPORARY ASSIGNMENTS

  • Always include a copy of the assignment letter with each expense report submitted.
  • Receipts for lodging are required regardless if you are being paid based on a lump sum basis when charged to a FAR applicable contract or overhead.
  • Separate general business expenses from living/travel expenses.
  • Lodging and Meal expenses shall be considered reasonable only to the extent that they do not exceed the daily maximum Government Per Diem rate in effect at the time of travel when charged to FAR applicable contracts or overhead.

MISCELLANEOUS

  • Laundry and dry cleaning expenses will be reimbursed if incurred while on a business trip over five days or when trips are unexpectedly extended.
  • Some travel expenses are considered personal and will not be reimbursed. Examples are:
    • Air travel insurance
    • Airline clubs
    • Fees for personal credit cards
    • Damage of personal property
    • Barber/hair stylist, manicures, massages etc..

SIGNATURES/APPROVALS

  • Each employee is responsible for the completeness and accuracy of each submittal.
  • It is never appropriate to enter another employee's JEMS work-queue to prepare or submit unless you a designated preparer or submitter.
