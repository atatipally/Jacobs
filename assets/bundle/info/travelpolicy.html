1.0          PURPOSE OF POLICY

It is the purpose of this policy to provide employees with a statement of Jacobs Engineering Group Inc. policies and procedures for the expenditures and accounting of Company funds used for business-related travel and entertainment.

2.0          POLICY OBJECTIVE

It is recognized that, for many employees, travel and entertainment expenses are incurred in order to further business objectives. The objective of this travel policy is to manage travel and entertainment costs in a manner that ensures cost-effectiveness, traveler safety, and consideration of traveler comfort.

3.0          SCOPE OF APPLICATION

This policy applies to all employees of Jacobs Engineering Group Inc., its operations and its subsidiaries.

4.0          POLICY

An employee traveling on Company business is expected to exercise the same care in incurring expenses as a prudent person traveling for personal reasons.  Travel on business should be conducted at minimum cost for achieving the success of the mission.

The need for standardization of both documentation and application of such expenses and the increasing reporting requirements of the U.S. Internal Revenue Service, similar taxing agencies of other countries and other regulatory bodies dictate that all employees of the Company are guided by a common set of expense policies.

It is the responsibility of each employee incurring such expenses and each supervisory person approving them to ensure that the policy guidelines are followed.  Jacobs Engineering Group Inc. has implemented exception reporting, which supports the travel policies, procedures and the preferred supplier programs.  Exception reporting by employee may be forwarded to the appropriate manager for review.

All employees who travel for business or arrange travel are expected to become acquainted with this policy as set forth herein.

4.1          GENERAL

Use of Designated Travel Agencies - Employees traveling on Company business shall use the currently designated Travel Agency or Online Booking Tool for their location for all business travel bookings.  BCD Travel is the designated agency in the United States of America and Cliqbook is the online booking tool.  For the most current country by country listing of travel agencies and approved booking sources, please refer to JNET Travel pages - Book Your Trip

Employees will be kept notified of any changes in travel agency designation.

Use of Jacobs' Designated Travel Agencies ensures all traveler data is transferred into Jacobs' ISOS Traveler Tracking Tool.

If for any reason, a designated Travel Agency cannot be used to make travel reservations, the employee is responsible for entering their complete travel itinerary (air, car and hotel) into ISOS’s Personal Travel Locator Tool, which will transfer the data into our the ISOS Traveler Tracking program.  The ISOS Personal Travel Locator can be found at https://www.internationalsos.com

Cost Containment/Policy Adherence - Managers are responsible for evaluating all trips to determine if the trip is necessary and cost effective.  For the purposes of this policy, the term "manager" or "management" will mean the employee’s direct supervisor or assigned project manager.

Travel cost in connection with work under contract will be charged to that job as a direct cost.  All other travel will be charged to overhead.  Cost saving options as identified in this policy applies equally to project and overhead paid travel.

If a client has a more stringent travel policy than this policy, the traveler should adhere to the client policy whenever possible.

Non-compliance with this policy may result in disciplinary action.

Employee’s travel that will be charged direct to a project must adhere to the terms and conditions of the governing contract.

Travel Risk Planner (TRiP) and SPA's - All travel requires completion of an SPA via use of Jacobs' Travel Risk Planner tool (TRiP) prior to trip commencement. TRiP is a web-enabled tool that provides the ability to complete a travel SPA with the appropriate approvals based on the travel risk ratings for the country you are visiting. Additionally, all international travel requires prior approval from a Group or Senior Vice President.  TRiP can be accessed at http://trip.jacobs.com

4.2          AIR TRAVEL

Two-Hour Window - It is the obligation of each employee to choose the least expensive flight including restricted fare options, from all alternatives within two (2) hours of the originally intended departure/arrival time. Within these parameters, any savings of $100.00 or less per round trip may be declined for any reason without approval.

Exception - Savings greater than $100.00 per round trip are designated as an exception.  All exceptions require written manager approval.  If the manager approves an exception, a report with this approval may be forwarded to senior management to review the savings forfeiture to the Company.

Connections/Alternate Airports - While non-stop flights are the prime objective, we expect travelers to accept one-stop or connecting flights (within the two-hour window parameter) if savings of $300 or greater per round trip are available.  Additionally, if alternate airports within a reasonable proximity of the requested departure/arrival city and within the 2 hour window parameter have a savings of $500 or greater round trip, travelers should accept the alternate airport.  Managers will need to compare employee travel savings versus additional travel time to determine if cost savings should be forfeited.

First Class/Business Class - On international flights, many airlines offer three classes of service, first class, business class and coach.  Coach class is the least expensive and should normally be used.  However, from time to time our clients will allow, and reimburse for business or first class for international flights.  You must have prior approval from a Group or Senior Vice President for first or business class travel. 

Upgrades - Are authorized only when obtained at no extra cost to the Company (i.e. a frequent flyer incentive offered by the airlines). Charges associated with Early Bird Check-in/Business Select or other airline “perks” are considered similar to upgrades and are not reimbursable.

Frequent Flyer Points - Travelers may retain frequent flyer points and awards for personal use.  Under no circumstances should any employee cause the Company to pay a higher airfare in order to fly on the carrier of his or her selection so that extra mileage credits and awards can be accrued.  The same criteria apply to hotel and rental cars.

Airline Transaction Fees - Per our U.S. agreement with BCD Travel, a transaction fee will be charged for each ticket generated.  There may also be costs associated with voids, exchanges, refunds and lost ticket transactions.  Plan ahead to avoid changes.  Agreements vary from country to country, please refer to the contacts listed on JNET - Book Your Trip for details.

Unused Airline Tickets – Unused airline tickets or vouchers issued by our designated Travel Agencies must be returned immediately for refunding and cancellation.  Unused tickets require notification by the traveler to the Travel Agency so that the redemption or refund can be obtained.  Do not exchange tickets at the airport unless the Travel Agency has instructed you to do so.  Never destroy or throw away airline tickets, as these are accountable documents.

Electronic Ticketing -The preferred method of ticketing is electronic ticketing or "E-ticketing".  Tickets will be issued in this form unless prohibited by the airline.

Airport Purchases - The purchase of airline tickets at the airport is not authorized except in emergency situations.  Use your country’s designated Travel Agency during business hours.  In the event of an emergency after hours, you may call your Travel Agency’s after hours service to purchase new tickets or to make changes in route.  A fee will be charged for each after hours call.   Refer to JNET - Book Your Trip for after hours contact information.

Charter Aircraft – Charter aircraft for Company business travel must be approved in advance at the Group/Senior Vice President level.  Use of personal aircraft for Company travel is prohibited.

Company Paid Spouse/Domestic Partner/Family – Company paid spouse/domestic partner/family travel must be authorized specifically on a travel authorization in advance of the trip.  A copy of the travel authorization must be included with the expense report before reimbursement can be made.

Airport Parking - The most economical available choice for airport parking is required.  When available, long-term parking should be used for trips over 24 hours.  Cab/Shuttle services to/from the airport should be utilized for extended travel when cost would be less than airport parking costs.

4.3          RAIL TRAVEL REQUIREMENTS

Rail travel is approved if it is the most economical and time efficient means of travel.

4.4          LODGING  

Jacobs Engineering Group Inc. has negotiated corporate rates with various individual properties and hotel chains, and has given consideration to the class of hotel depending upon where an employee is in the world.  The most current version of Jacobs’ Preferred Hotel Listing can be found on JNET - Book Your Trip

All Hotel reservations should be booked through your country’s Designated Travel Agency or via Cliqbook and not the hotel.    This allows Jacobs to track room nights for lower negotiated rates based on volume.  It also ensures hotel data is captured in the ISOS Traveller Tracking tool.  Exceptions must be entered manually in the Personal Travel Locator portal, located on ISOS's main page at https://www.internationalsos.com

All personal valuables should be left at home or stored in a hotel safe.  Neither Jacobs Engineering Group Inc. nor the hotel is responsible for valuables left in your room.

Hotel Guarantees/Cancellations - All reservations are guaranteed for late arrival. Guarantees will be applied to the traveler’s corporate credit card.  Employees should understand the hotel’s cancellation policy and plan accordingly; hotel cancellation policies vary from property to property.  If a reservation needs to be cancelled the employee is responsible for canceling it within the timeframe specified by that hotel’s cancellation policy to avoid any “no-show” penalties.  Employees must contact the travel department or the hotel directly and obtain a cancellation number as documentation of the transaction.

Long term stays - Prolonged stays of two weeks or longer should be reviewed with your country’s designated Travel Agency with as much advance notice as possible.  Volume discounts could possibly be negotiated.

Stays of 30 days or more might be made more economical by utilizing Jacobs’ long-term housing partner, Oakwood Temporary Housing.  Oakwood Temporary Housing provides Jacobs long-term housing solutions on a global basis.  For more information, refer to our customized website at www.Oakwood.com.

Meeting Space - All contracts associated with room blocks, banquet facilities, etc. negotiated directly with a hotel must be reviewed by your Legal Department prior to signing.  No exceptions.  Be aware of the cancellation policy and any penalty fees associated with cancelling or modifying your meeting.  No individual is permitted to use their personal credit card for payment of a group or to collect loyalty points or mileage related to group stays. 

4.5          RENTAL CARS

All rental cars should be reserved through your country’s Designated Travel Agency. Jacobs Engineering Group Inc. has negotiated rates with HERTZ as our primary preferred vendor.  Secondary preferred vendors are National Car Rental and Enterprise Rent-A-Car. (Enterprise is only contracted for use in the U. S., Puerto Rico and Canada). The Company authorizes rentals of intermediate or compact class automobiles only. Full-size rental cars are allowed when entertaining or transporting more than four individuals and it is more economical for the Company. Any exception to these requirements must be approved in advance by the manager.  

It is against policy to rent vehicles under another company or association name when traveling on Company business.

The employee will need to meet the age and driving requirements of the car rental company.  Fellow employees may legally drive the car if these requirements are met.  This may vary by location.  Contact your country’s Designated Travel Agency for details.

To avoid inflated gasoline charges, always refuel your vehicle before returning it to the rental location. 

You are encouraged to rent GPS equipment only when the complexity of your driving itinerary is such that this type of assistance is required or driving conditions would be compromised without the provision of GPS.

Loss Damage Waiver (LDW) covers damages to the rental vehicle, including theft, vandalism and collision.  Hertz, National and Enterprise provide no-cost LDW to Jacobs Engineering Group Inc. travelers using the Jacobs Engineering Group Inc. corporate agreement account number, and renting at contracted rates.  LDW coverage for Hertz and National applies at all corporate and participating licensee locations in the U.S, Puerto Rico, Canada, Mexico and Western Europe as long as the renter does not violate any terms of the rental agreement.   Coverage varies, but is also available in other countries, check with your Designated Travel Agency for details.  Your Travel Agency will notify you if you are renting from a non- participating location.  

DECLINE LDW when renting from preferred providers in the U.S. and from participating locations globally. ACCEPT LDW when renting from non-participating locations of Hertz, National and Enterprise or an approved alternate company.

Liability Insurance -Liability insurance covers persons and property other than the renter.  Hertz, National and Enterprise provide Jacobs Engineering Group Inc. travelers using the Jacobs Engineering Group Inc. corporate agreement account number and renting at contract rates, no-cost liability insurance.  This Liability coverage applies at all corporate and participating licensee locations of Hertz and National in the U.S., Puerto Rico, Canada, and Western Europe.

DECLINE Liability insurance when renting from preferred providers, unless otherwise advised by the Travel Company. ACCEPT liability coverage when renting from non-participating locations of Hertz, National and Enterprises or an approved alternative company.

If the traveler is unsure as to insurance options, they should confirm with the Travel Agency to determine the proper election at the time the reservation is requested.

In case of an accident, the renter should:

§  Call the police for an official report

§  Notify the rental car agency immediately.  The number to call is provided on the jacket included with the rental agreement

§  Obtain the name, address, and phone number of any other parties involved in the accident.

§  Complete the accident form that is printed on the inside of the rental jacket.

§  Notify the Company's Risk Management department immediately at (877) 832-1721.

§  The Company assumes no responsibility for a fine assessed against any employee for a moving violation, parking violation, or other vehicle-related offense while driving on Company business.

U.S.-based renters get unlimited mileage when renting on the Jacobs Engineering Group Inc. corporate rate at all corporate and participating licensee locations in the U.S. Additional charges apply on one-way rentals.  These will vary and employees should contact BCD Travel or consult Cliqbook for details.

U.S.-based travelers should use the Hertz GOLD or National Emerald Aisle program (express service). Jacobs’ employees are eligible for a fee waived membership.  Contact BCD Travel for information and instructions to enroll.

Use of Personal Vehicles - If an employee chooses to use a personal vehicle on Company business, the employee will be reimbursed at the mileage rate established by the Company or governing contract.  This reimbursement is intended to cover all the costs associated with the operation and maintenance of the vehicle including but not limited to gasoline, oil, tires, damage to the vehicle, insurance and deductibles.  Employee owned vehicles are not authorized to be used on Company business unless the vehicle is covered by current liability insurance with minimum coverage consistent with state requirements.  Employees are responsible for ensuring that they carry the appropriate liability insurance for personal vehicles that are used primarily for Company business.   All accidents involving employee owned vehicles being used on Company business should be reported to your personal insurance company and notify the Company risk management department immediately at (877) 832-1721.  Jacobs Engineering Group Inc. does not cover damage to the employee vehicle while conducting Company business; however, medical care/reimbursement may be covered through workers' compensation.

4.6          TRAVEL REIMBURSEMENT

Regular travelers are to use Company provided American Express credit cards for travel and apply for reimbursement upon returning from their trip.  Failure to use the Company provided credit card might result in delayed reimbursement.   Airfare may be expensed prior to travel to ensure the employee is not burdened with outstanding charges prior to being reimbursed by Jacobs.

In the event an employee incurs a charge on their corporate card that is pending refund, they will place those costs in dispute pending receipt of a new statement.  If the charge remains open on a second statement, employee will notify the Designated Travel Agency and Corporate Card Administrator for resolution.  The Designated Travel Agency upon notification will process all refunds by the traveler, and the Designated Travel Agency will confirm refund initiation.

New hires or infrequent travelers who do not have Company provided credit cards are eligible for advances up to a maximum of $200 per day to cover hotel/meals and miscellaneous items.

Expense reports should be turned in within seven days of incurring the expense or within 7 days of completing travel with all advanced monies cleared.  Additional advance monies will not be issued to any employee who has outstanding advances for greater than 30 days.

4.7          MISCELLANEOUS

Meals - Unless per diem governs, the traveler will be reimbursed the actual reasonable costs for meals plus taxes and tips on business trips away from the home office.

Laundry and Dry Cleaning Service - Reasonable laundry and dry cleaning expenses incurred during business trips over five days or when trips are unexpectedly extended are reimbursable.

International Travel - International documentation costs such as visas, passports, immunizations, etc. will be reimbursed at actual reasonable costs.

Receipts - When possible, obtain receipts for all expenses incurred.  Unless reimbursable projects, your group or country require a lower figure, provide receipts for all individual expenses of $75 or more. Receipts for air, hotel and car are required irrespective of dollar value.

Preferred Vendors - Corporate rates have been negotiated with a number of air, hotel, and rental car vendors.  Employees are encouraged to use these preferred vendors unless their services are unavailable, or other comparable options are available at lower rates (Bear in mind our rental car rates are inclusive of insurance). Preferred vendor information can be found on JNET’s Travel Pages. Please refer to JNET Travel pages Travel/Book Your Trip, or by consulting your country's designated Travel Agency, or Cliqbook.
