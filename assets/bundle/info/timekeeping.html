Policy Overview

Employees are responsible for properly coding their attendance or absence, recording hours worked, properly using charge numbers, and consulting with supervisors if they have questions.  Management is responsible for ensuring employees understand their timekeeping responsibilities and providing annual refresher training and the information needed to properly record their time. 

A timesheet is a legal document, subject to audit, and certified as accurate when submitted. Accurate recording is every employee’s personal responsibility and a continuing condition of employment. Accuracy is demonstrated when a timesheet is reasonably free from error and there is an appropriate relationship among (a) time spent, (b) activities worked (projects, overhead, etc.), and (c) time recorded.  The risk of inaccuracy increases with the number of projects/tasks worked and the time elapsed between the time the work is performed and the entering of the time on the timesheet.

To reduce the risk of inaccuracy and associated regulatory problems we have developed the following simple requirements:

 

·       All Employees shall record their time on a daily basis in ETS.  This includes those employees charging single or multiple projects and single or multiple overhead or indirect cost accounts.

·       All employees shall complete their time entries for the previous day by no later than 10:00 am on the following day.

·       Pre-completion of time or pre-submission of your timesheet is not permitted, except when the employee is out of the office for a day or two or for an extended period on business travel, personal time off, or when on leave and ETS is inaccessible. Employees should explain the circumstances (travel, unavailability, leaves, etc.) for pre-completing a timesheet in the comment field.

·       Completed timesheets shall be submitted for approval by the employee into the timekeeping system (ETS) at the start of the business day each Friday unless Payroll requires earlier submission due to holiday work schedules or your office is on different work schedule. 

·       Employees may explain unusual circumstances (e.g., explanation for corrections, etc.) in the comment field.

·       Labor corrections shall be prepared when necessary to accurately reflect time incurred, e.g., correcting inaccuracies due to time estimates, charging the incorrect project number, etc.  Corrections after submission and approval require a properly completed labor correction form. 


Supervisors shall review and approve time charges for their employees.  It is the Supervisors responsibility to assure that their employees complete and submit their time in accordance with this policy and to approve the submitted time in ETS or by signature on paper documents when ETS is not available. On an exception basis, this duty may be delegated to a secondary approver who has knowledge of the employee’s attendance and labor distribution. 

ETS is the company’s primary tool for recording and entering time. If ETS is inaccessible, a supplemental record is permitted but only for the period of inaccessibility. As soon as practical, the employee shall enter time into ETS consistent with the supplemental record. A timesheet coordinator is permitted to complete the timesheet when the employee is unavailable and submission is necessary to meet payroll-processing deadlines.

 

Violations of this policy are subject to the Progressive Discipline Policy 5-302 and may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.
