California Meal and Rest Periods
Timekeeping

Revision & Effective Date:

Issuing Department:

Accounting

Previous Revision & Effective Date:

  • 1.0 PURPOSE

    The purpose of this policy is to define Jacobs Engineering Group Inc., its subsidiaries and affiliated companies (hereinafter, "the Company") practices in California in order to appropriately account for time and pay of hourly employees as related to meal and rest periods.

  • 2.0 SCOPE

    This policy applies to all non-exempt Jacobs employees working in the state of California.

  • 3.0 POLICY

    • 3.1 The Company's meal and rest period policy shall be interpreted and applied in accordance with California state requirements.

      • 3.1.1 Rest Periods

        Non-exempt employees working in California are entitled to a rest period of ten minutes net rest time as follows:

        Hours of WorkRest Periods
        0 - <3.50
        3.5 - 61
        > 6 - 102
        >10 - 143
        >14 - 184

        Managers should plan work to provide rest periods and are not to discourage the taking of rest periods or incentivize not taking rest periods. If a non-exempt employee is precluded from taking one or more rest periods during the work week, the employee is entitled to one additional hour of pay at the regular rate of pay for each day that precluded from taking one or more rest periods. In such a case, an employee is to record an additional one hour on the day(s) one or more rest periods were not taken, to Project 520000 (Overhead) and Pay Type "NO MEAL/REST." If and employee is precluded from taking all rest and all meal periods in a day (regardless of how many) they would bill an additional two hours to "No MEAL/REST". The additional hour(s) of pay is not work time and does not increase the number of overtime hours worked.

        If a non-exempt employee voluntarily chooses to waive all of the rest periods that were not taken during a day, the employee will not be eligible to receive any additional pay for that day.

        Rest periods should, insofar as practicable, be in the middle of each work period, such as between the beginning of work and a meal period, between two meal periods or between the last meal period and the end of work for the day. Rest periods can be taken at other times with supervisor approval when not practical to have in the middle of the work period. However, employees may not to report late and/or early during a scheduled work day in lieu of taking rest periods.

      • 3.1.2 Meal Periods

        An employee is not to work more than five hours, including the rest period, without having the opportunity to start a 30-minute, off-duty, unpaid meal period during which the employee can leave the premises unless the total work period for any given day is no more than 6 hours. An employee is not to work more than ten hours, including the rest periods, without having the opportunity to start a second, 30-minute, off-duty, unpaid meal period during which the employee can leave the premises. A non-exempt employee may waive a meal period which occurs close to the end of an employee's workday with the advance approval of the employee's supervisor.

        Managers should plan work to provide meal periods and are not to discourage the taking of meal periods or incentivize not taking meal periods. Any meal period that was less than 30 minutes in duration or during which a non-exempt employee was not relieved of all duty must be recorded and paid as work time. If a non-exempt employee is precluded from taking one or more timely meal periods of sufficient duration during the work week, the employee is entitled to one additional hour of pay at the regular rate of pay for each day that he/she is precluded from taking one or more meal periods. Record an additional one hour on the day(s) one or more meal periods(s) were not taken, to Project 520000 (Overhead) and Pay Type "NO MEAL/REST". The additional hour of pay is not work time and does not increase the number of overtime hours worked. If a non-exempt employee voluntarily chooses to work through all of the meal periods that were not taken during a day, the employee will not be entitled to receive an additional hour of pay for missing meal periods on that day.

      • 3.1.3 Timesheet Certification

        Upon timesheet submission in ETS, a non-exempt employee will be required to certify that all applicable meal and rest periods for the week were provided, were provided and voluntarily waived, or were missed. A non-exempt employee must record the additional hour(s) as described above in Section 3.1.1 and 3.1.2. This certification will be required of all non-exempt employees on a weekly basis.

        Questions regarding work time, time recording, compensation for time worked, rest periods and meal periods should be directed to your supervisor or if the employee wishes to Human Resources (JESI) Jacobs at 800-957-5374 or JFSNA at 877-287-1811. It is the Company's policy that all work time be recorded accurately, that rest and meal periods be taken as described above, and that employees be paid promptly and in full. It is the Company's policy that the inclusion of hours not worked or the failure to accurately record all hours worked on a time record are serious offenses and that the employee making such entries as well as any supervisor or manager who directs or tolerates such entries is subject to discipline up to and including termination of employment.

      Employees who believe they are being asked or directed to record inaccurate timekeeping information, including time related to meal and rest periods, are expected to report such conduct to company management, Human Resources or the Integrity Hotline Jacobs Integrity Hotline Callers in North America, (877) JACOBS2 (877-522-6272. All others, use their country-specific AT &T Direct Access number followed by (877) JACOBS2 (877-522-6272)

    • 3.2 Training

      Impacted employees and managers will be provided training on this policy at the time of hire and periodically thereafter. Participation in this training is required.

    • 3.3 Audits

      The Internal Audit department will review employee status relating to this policy and report exceptions to Human Resources and Accounting and Finance.

    • 3.4 Corrective Action

      The violation of this policy, including the following misconduct, may result in corrective action, up to and including termination of employment:

      1. The falsification of any time record or the alteration of a time record by an employee or a supervisor such that the time record is inaccurate;
      2. Performing work without recording the work time in accordance with this policy;
      3. A supervisor's directing, permitting or tolerating a non-exempt employee to work off the clock or to work in violation of applicable meal and rest period requirements; and
      4. Retaliating or discriminating against a non-exempt employee for accurately reporting all work time or raising a concern regarding the reporting of work time and meal and rest periods or regarding the employee's compensation or another employee's compensation.
      5. The failure of a manager to plan work to provide meal and rest periods whenever possible.
      6. A manager's coercing, discouraging or incentivizing employees to not take meal and rest periods.

  • 4.0 MAINTENANCE

    Accounting and Finance will review this policy periodically against applicable regulations and update as needed.
